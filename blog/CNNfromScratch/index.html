CNN from Scratch

A case study on building a convolutional neural network with only NumPy, including manual convolution, backpropagation, and enough debugging to make the abstractions feel earned.

Table of Contents

Overview

This project was about understanding the full pipeline instead of leaning on a framework to hide the machinery. I implemented convolution, ReLU, max pooling, flattening, dense layers, softmax, and backpropagation directly in Python and NumPy.

Why It Was Worth Doing

Modern frameworks are great for shipping models faster, but they also make it easy to skip the details that explain why the model works at all. Implementing the pieces manually forced careful thinking about dimensions, gradients, numerical stability, and where performance actually gets lost.

What I Built

  • Convolution layers with forward and backward passes.
  • ReLU and max pooling.
  • Dense layers, softmax, and cross-entropy loss.
  • Training logic for MNIST.

What I Learned

The hardest part was not writing matrix operations. It was debugging gradients and keeping the bookkeeping clean. The project also reinforced something I like about implementation-first work in general: once you build the details yourself, the abstraction stops feeling magical.
