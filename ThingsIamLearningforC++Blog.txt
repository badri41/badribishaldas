

1. Why does the Destructor of a Base Class has to be virtual? 

-> Destructors are not overriden in the traditional sense of the word, When you override a class method,
the only most derived method is called in the class hierarchy. When you do the same with Destructors all
Destructors in the hierarchy are called.. (Need to dive deeper what does this still mean)

-> C++ Idiom "Destructors should either be public and virtual, or protected and non-virtual"

-> Diff between public Inheritance and private Inheritance
Private Inheritance (default for class)

Formal definition:
All public and protected members of A become private in B.

A::public → becomes private in B
A::protected → becomes private in B
A::private → still inaccessible

Ex: 
Class A {}
Class B : public A {}
